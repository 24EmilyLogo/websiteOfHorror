Practical Effects vs Computer Generated Effects
(And how to make VFX injuries)

Practical Both Computer Generated
  • Tactile, can touch the thing and see the thing, making it easier to be in character
  • There is only so much that someone can make using things around especially if there is a tight budget
  • Already have a base to work off of and touch up with the practical take
  • Takes about double the time of either option

  • Can be confusing if the person has no experience with editing software

What are practical and computer generated effects?

Practical effects are physical effects created by something other than a computer. Where as computer generated effects are digitally applied effects after the footage has been taken. Practical effects are good because it's easier to react to something you can feel or see in process. On the other hand, digital effects are good because digital effects can clean up any messy takes. It's best to use both because there is a base for the digital from the practical effects. Also any mistakes made in the practical effect take can be cleaned up and look smoother and more closer to the end goal.
